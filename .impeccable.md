## Design Context

### Users
Levain is for home bakers managing live fermentations on iPhone, usually in the kitchen and often with limited attention. The main job is not studying recipes but knowing what needs action now, what is planned next, and how starter or batch maintenance fits into the day.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, reliable. The product should feel like a lightweight operational companion: native, trustworthy, and clear under time pressure. It should never feel playful, decorative, or dashboard-heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode only. SF Pro. Planta-inspired large cards, generous whitespace, Apple-style hierarchy, continuous squircles, and a planner-first layout. The visual system is semantic before decorative: green for action or plan, neutral for archived or supporting structure, red for problems and destructive flows.

### Design Principles
- Action-first: every primary screen should answer "what do I need to do now?" before showing supporting information.
- Semantic color discipline: green means present or planned action, neutral means past or secondary context, red means problem; avoid decorative status colors.
- Native operational clarity: prefer readable cards, direct actions, and iPhone-native patterns over custom chrome or novelty.
- Progressive disclosure: keep advanced controls and detailed metadata behind the primary operational flow.
- Spacious hierarchy: use generous breathing room, consistent card padding, and strong typography so urgency is obvious without visual noise.
